    A creative writers greatest strength is in their ability and wililngness to read; this is
    something I wholeheartedly believe to be true, and as such I make it my goal to be deliberate
    in my readings of narratives of all forms. As someone taken by Video Games as a narrative form,
    I often find myself wishing to express where in these texts I find myself uniquely touched or
    moved - this has lead to the development of written and video-form essays on some of my
    favourite narrative experiences.
  • The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask - To Deny a Legacy
    The world of Termina, doomed to fall by the whims of an ancient evil
    and the all consuming moon they conjure, has only one saviour, on hero. But, in a world where
    none know his name or his deeds, did this hero ever really exist?
  • Sonic Frontiers - Finding Your Flame
    Sonic Frontiers is a game of many narratives - a narrative of success as
    a well received and supported modern sonic game; a narrative of isolation, struggling against
    vulnerability and weakness to find the strength to fight on; and a narrative of exploration and
    discovery, as an open world sonic experience. But for me, Sonic Frontiers is best viewed as a
    narrative about the facets of identity. Sonic and his friends feel, see and learn a lot during
    their adventures across the Starfall Islands - most importantly, they learn what it really means
    to Find Your Flame.
